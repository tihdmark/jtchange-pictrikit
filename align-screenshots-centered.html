Centered alignment makes screenshots look balanced and intentional. When an image sits perfectly in the middle of its space, it draws attention to the content rather than the arrangement. But getting that perfect center manually is surprisingly frustrating.

filter_center_focus

Why centering matters

Off-center images feel unfinished. Even a few pixels of misalignment can make a layout look careless. When you're sharing screenshots for work, documentation, or social media, that small detail affects how professional your content appears. Centered images look deliberate. Off-center images look like mistakes.

The frustration of manual centering

Most tools let you drag images around freely, which sounds flexible but creates problems. You nudge an image left, then right, then left again. You zoom in to check if it's really centered. You use alignment guides that sometimes help and sometimes don't. For something so basic, it takes too much effort.

Automatic centering that just works

This tool centers every screenshot automatically. When you place an image into a layout slot, it is centered by defaultboth horizontally and vertically. You do not drag, you do not nudge, you do not guess. The image goes where it belongs, and it stays there.

What you can do

With automatic centering, you can:

  • Place screenshots that are always perfectly centered
  • Combine multiple images with consistent alignment
  • Skip the manual positioning entirely
  • Get balanced results every time
